## 🔥 Breakthrough Findings

### 1. The Marconi Myth (Exp 16)
Every physics textbook says Marconi's 1901 transatlantic signal bounced off the ionosphere. It didn't. At 820 kHz during midday, D-layer absorption makes skywave propagation impossible. It was ground wave over seawater. Tesla knew something was off.

### 2. Longitudinal Waves Were Real (Exp 18)
The century-long dismissal of Tesla's "non-Hertzian longitudinal waves" was wrong. TM₀ guided modes on the Earth-ionosphere waveguide inherently have longitudinal E-field components. "EM waves are transverse" only applies to unbounded free-space — not guided waves on conductors.

### 3. Wardenclyffe Would Have Worked (Exp 15)
Not for power delivery — but as a global communication system, Wardenclyffe was sound engineering. It would have beaten Marconi to reliable transatlantic service.

### 4. You Can Build One (Exp 19)
A functional Earth-ionosphere exciter — not a toy Tesla coil, but a real VLF transmitter operating on the same physics as Colorado Springs — costs about $2,000 in parts and is detectable at 1,000 km.
